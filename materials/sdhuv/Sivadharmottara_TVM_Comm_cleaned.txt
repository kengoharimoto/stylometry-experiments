/NOTE
It is difficult to understand the sentence mātāpitroś ca svakaṇṭhoktatvāt. In the preceding passages, the commentator gives a sophisticated explanation about the words deva, agni and guru in 7.11a (devāgniguruviprāṇāṃ). The purport of his discussions was that the words deva, agni and guru do not include Śiva, Śivāgni, and Śivamantropadeṣṭṛ respectively and that those who worship them will immediately go to Śivaloka, not Yamaloka like normal pious people. In the case of mother and father, there is no need to postulate that mother and father refer to something related to Śiva. Therefore, we understand that the author intends to say that worshippers of mother and father are literally those of mother and father because it is clearly stated (ukta) from the mouth (kaṇṭha) of the author of the mūla text himself (sva).
